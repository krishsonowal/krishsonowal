Project Overview

Health and Orange is an e-commerce website specialising in edible cold-pressed oils within the health and nutrition niche. When the project began, the site had minimal organic presence — receiving just 12 clicks and 531 impressions per month from Google Search.

Within under 4 months, the website achieved 500 monthly clicks, 23,600+ impressions, and visibility across 870+ keywords including high-intent commercial queries directly tied to their products.

The Challenge

The brand operated in a competitive health and wellness space where established authority sites dominate. The site had no existing content strategy, minimal on-page optimisation, and weak internal linking — meaning Google had little reason to surface it for relevant searches.

The Strategy

Keyword Research & Targeting: Identifying low-competition, high-intent keywords around walnut oil, cold-pressed oils, and related health benefits — terms real buyers were searching for but where large sites weren't dominating.

On-Page SEO & Content Optimisation: Every key product page and article was rewritten to align with user search intent, improving title tags, meta descriptions, headings, and body content.

Internal Linking: A deliberate structure was built to pass authority between pages, improve crawlability, and guide visitors naturally through product discovery.

Search Intent Alignment: Each page was evaluated for buyer journey stage and content shaped to attract qualified, converting traffic.

The Results

Organic clicks grew from 12 to 500 (a 4,066% increase). Impressions jumped from 531 to over 23,600. Average position improved from 10.2 to 7.1, pushing multiple keywords from page 2 to page 1. The site now ranks for 870+ keywords including branded, product-specific, and informational searches.

Project Overview

Internify is a platform focused on internships and student opportunities, targeting university students searching for internships and career-related resources. When the SEO engagement began, the platform was generating moderate traffic but was largely stuck on page 2 of Google.

Over 3 months, a structured campaign pushed the platform firmly onto page 1 — growing monthly clicks from 1,290 to 2,860, improving average position from 10.1 to 5.9, and raising CTR from 7.7% to 10.1%.

The Challenge

Internify operated where larger job boards dominate generic searches. The challenge was carving out a specific niche around student and university internship searches. CTR was also underperforming, suggesting titles and descriptions weren't compelling enough to earn clicks even when appearing in results.

The Strategy

Student-Specific Keyword Targeting: Focusing on highly specific, student-intent queries like "fyugp internship", "internship for fyugp students", and location-based variations such as "internify kochi" — terms with lower competition but high relevance.

On-Page SEO Optimisation: Key landing pages were restructured with improved title tags, meta descriptions, and body content. Titles and descriptions were rewritten to directly address student needs and stand out against generic job board listings.

Internal Linking & Intent Alignment: Improved internal linking distributed authority more effectively, while content was reshaped to deliver on user intent immediately.

The Results

Monthly organic clicks grew from 1,290 to 2,860 (+121%). Impressions expanded from 16,800 to 28,400. Average position improved from 10.1 to 5.9. CTR improved from 7.7% to 10.1%, reflecting strong alignment between the site's content and student search behaviour. The platform now ranks for 700+ keywords.

Project Overview

LedgersCFO is a finance and consulting website focused on services including CFO support, tax consulting, and business advisory. When the project began, the site had a very low average position of 60.8 — buried deep in Google results — generating just 127 clicks per month despite having 2,740 impressions.

Within 3 months, organic traffic more than doubled to 268 clicks, average position improved dramatically to 20.2, and CTR tripled from 4.6% to 14.9%.

Understanding the Numbers

Total impressions decreased from 2,740 to 1,800 — a positive signal. It reflects a deliberate shift away from broad, low-relevance search appearances toward more targeted, high-intent queries where LedgersCFO genuinely belongs. Fewer irrelevant impressions, far more meaningful clicks.

The Challenge

Professional services firms like LedgersCFO face a specific SEO challenge: the audience is small, highly specific, and searching with very particular language. A founder searching for fractional CFO support uses very different terms than someone looking for a general accountant. The site also had a very low starting position (60.8 average), indicating Google had little confidence in the pages' relevance.

The Strategy

Financial Keyword Targeting: Research focused on the specific language used by LedgersCFO's target clients — founders, business owners, and finance decision-makers. This surfaced high-intent terms like "due diligence for founders", "fp&a for fractional cfo", and branded queries like "ledgerscfo".

On-Page SEO & Content Optimisation: Service pages were restructured and rewritten to directly address the queries and pain points of the intended audience. Title tags and meta descriptions were rewritten to be both keyword-relevant and compelling enough to earn clicks.

Site Structure & Intent Alignment: The website structure was refined to clearly communicate what the firm does and who it serves, reinforcing topical authority in the finance and advisory space.

The Results

Organic traffic grew from 127 to 268 clicks (+110%). Average position improved from 60.8 to 20.2. CTR tripled from 4.6% to 14.9%, reflecting strong alignment between the site's content and what searchers were looking for. The site began ranking for new finance keywords including branded, founder-specific, and service-level searches.

Project Overview

P3 India is an e-commerce apparel brand where the focus was on improving product visibility, keyword rankings, and overall organic search performance. The engagement ran for approximately 3–4 weeks, from 26 May to 16 June — a tight timeline that required a precise, high-impact approach rather than a broad, long-term strategy.

In that short window, one key product page moved from position 65.1 to 7.2 on Google, a single product experienced a 35,800% increase in views, and sitewide organic traffic grew by 13.44% — demonstrating how quickly targeted SEO can move the needle when applied to the right pages.

The Challenge

E-commerce apparel is a highly competitive space, with large marketplaces and established fashion brands dominating most category searches. For a brand like P3 India, the path to organic growth isn't competing head-on with giants — it's finding the specific product and keyword opportunities where the site can rank and win.

The core challenge was identifying which product pages had the most potential for rapid ranking improvement, and executing on-page changes precise enough to move the needle within weeks rather than months.

The Strategy

High-Priority Product Page Optimisation: Rather than spreading effort across the entire catalogue, the strategy focused on identifying the product pages with the greatest ranking potential — pages close to breaking onto page 1, or where targeted keyword adjustments could produce the biggest jump. This focus is what enabled the key product page to move from position 65 to 7 in such a short time.

Keyword Targeting & On-Page Refinement: Product titles, descriptions, and metadata were refined to better match the specific search queries P3 India's target customers were using — not just generic category terms, but specific intent-driven queries that real shoppers use when searching for apparel.

Search Intent Alignment: Product pages were evaluated and updated to reflect what a user expects to find when landing from a search result — clear product information, relevant keywords in the right places, and a structure that signals relevance to Google's ranking systems.

The Results

For the key target product page, organic clicks grew from 5 to 11, impressions increased, and average search position improved dramatically from 65.1 to 7.2 — moving the product from near-invisible to page 1 on Google.

At the product level, one item experienced a 35,800% increase in views, with other products also showing growth of over 200%, indicating that improved discoverability was lifting the catalogue more broadly.

At the sitewide level, Google Analytics data showed 8,754 engaged sessions and a 13.44% increase in organic search traffic — consistent, measurable growth driven entirely by SEO improvements made in under a month.
